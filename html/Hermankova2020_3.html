Horse Rider plate fragment

Description: The stone plate preserved from 25 % with dimensions (m) w: 0.17 × h: 0.17 × d:0.045. In the lower right corner of the surviving part of the plate is clearly visible a shallow relief depicting a human foot and a horse hoof. The Horse Rider is facing right and sitting on a galloping horse. Relief depicting: Human figure, Horse
Text: No letters visible.
Letters: NA

Date: AD 50 - AD 217
Type of inscription: votive inscription, funerary inscription

Findspot: Yurta-Stroyno, Stroyno, Jambol, Bulgaria. Found at Yurta-Stroyno in 2015 incorporated into the foundation wall [SU_002] of the Room D, was reused as a building material at the time of the house construction in the begining of the 3rd c. AD (Tušlová et al. 2016, 257).
Original location: Unknown place in Thracia
Last recorded location: Stroyno Archaeological Project, now stored at Regional Historical Museum, Yambol, Bulgaria.
