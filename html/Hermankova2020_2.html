Stone plate fragment to/by the Avilius family member

Description: The stone plate preserved from 30 % with dimensions (m) w: 0.12 × h: 0.10 × d:0.04. The shallow relief originally represented either a Horse Rider scene facing right or a standing barefoot person. Relief depicting: Human figure
Text: Text located under the relief, the beginning of the text is missing.
Letters: Carefully carved with chisel letters with serifs. Letter Λ dimensions (m) 0.012 0.018

Date: AD 50 - AD 399
Type of inscription: votive inscription, funerary inscription

Findspot: Yurta-Stroyno, Stroyno, Jambol, Bulgaria. Found during archaeological investigation of the site Yurta-Stroyno in 2007. Bakardzhiev 2008, 471-2.
Original location: Unknown place in Thracia.
Last recorded location: Regional Historical Museum, Yambol, Bulgaria.

Interpretive edition:

Αβιλλιου

Diplomatic edition:

ΑΒΙΛΛΙΟΥ

apparatus

1: Double lambda instead of a single one.

Translation:

Commentary:

The text in the genitive singular reads ‘ΑΒΙΛΛΙΟΥ’, associating the dedicand or the deceased from the monument with the Roman family name Avilius. Related family name is recorded in the military camp at Kabyle, ca. 40 km north, see IG Bulg 3.2 1777
