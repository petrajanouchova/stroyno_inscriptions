Inscribed Roman brick

Description: The stone plate preserved from 85 % with dimensions (m) w: 0.155 × h: 0.28 × d:0.045. Large part of a rectangular brick, made of red clay with a short inscription. The brick was decorated with the half-letter-X shaped lines made with four fingers before firing, a typical decoration for Roman bricks.
Text: Short inscription is in the middle of the brick with available space around the text indicating it is unlikely any letters are missing.
Letters: The brick was inscribed with one finger before firing while the clay was still soft, leaving strokes of uneven depths.

Date: AD 50 - AD 399
Type of inscription: votive inscription

Findspot: Yurta-Stroyno, Stroyno, Jambol, Bulgaria. Found at Yurta-Stroyno in 2016 during the systematic surface survey of the site in the square G09.
Original location: Thracia
Last recorded location: Stroyno Archaeological Project, now stored at Regional Historical Museum, Yambol, Bulgaria.

Interpretive edition:

ἡ θεός

Diplomatic edition:

ΗΘΕΟΣ

apparatus

1: A variant reading suggests an adjective ᾔθεος with the subscript iota omitted.

Translation:

[Dedicated to] female-deity.

Commentary:

The text reads ‘ΗΘΕΟC’ and can be interpreted as a dedication to an unknown female deity, represented by the noun θεός in nominative singular with the definite article ἡ. A variant reading suggests an adjective ᾔθεος, ‘unmarried youth’ with the subscript iota omitted. In this case the adjective would serve as an epithet or a simple acclamation.
