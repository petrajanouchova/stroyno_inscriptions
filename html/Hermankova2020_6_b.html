Pottery graffiti

Description: The clay vessel fragment with dimensions (mm) × diam.:40 (Inner diameter of the base).
Text: Letters incised into the body immediately above the base of the vessel, on the outer side at the base of a jug
Letters: 15(mm), letter eta.

Date: AD 100 - AD 399
Type of inscription: Owner / artist inscription, Graffiti

Findspot: Yurta-Stroyno, Stroyno, Yambol, Bulgaria. Found at Yurta-Stroyno in 2015 in the robber's trench soil in the inner area of the house that was built after AD 217.
Original location: Unknown place in Thracia
Last recorded location: Stroyno Archaeological Project, now stored at Regional Historical Museum, Yambol, Bulgaria.

Interpretive edition:

η

Diplomatic edition:

Η

apparatus

1: First letter most likely Η

Translation:

NA

Commentary:

The textual graffiti containing individual letter of possibly Greek alphabet and is located on the outer side at the base of a jug, traditionally interpreted as an indication of the ownership.
